# Analyst Commentary: HMT Departmental Expenditure (2023)

## Summary

Departmental expenditure for HMRC, DWP, and MOD remained broadly stable throughout 2023, with all three departments showing a gradual upward trend. One significant anomaly was identified: an unexplained spike in HMRC expenditure in August 2023. One data quality issue (a missing GDP growth figure for DWP in August) limits the completeness of the analysis for that month.

## Key trends

- **DWP expenditure is consistently the highest**, ranging from £8,050m to £8,310m across the year. This reflects DWP's large welfare programme commitments and is in line with expectations.
- **HMRC shows steady growth from January to December**, rising from £4,200m to £4,340m — a 3.3% increase over the year. This is unremarkable and consistent with inflation-adjusted planning assumptions.
- **August 2023 is the outlier month.** HMRC expenditure spiked to £5,400m, 23.6% above its annual average. This warrants immediate investigation.
- **MOD spending is stable and unremarkable**, ranging narrowly from £3,570m to £3,660m. No anomalies were flagged. Defence expenditure shows no significant trend change across the year.
- **GDP growth was modest but positive throughout** (0.2%–0.6%), with one exception: the DWP August entry is missing a GDP figure, which limits any spending-to-growth analysis for that month.

## Anomalies and flags

HMRC reported expenditure of £5,400m in August 2023. The department's average across all twelve months is £4,370m, making the August figure 23.6% above the norm. Possible explanations include a one-off payment (such as a large tax settlement or refund programme), a data entry error, or a mid-year budget reallocation. This should be cross-referenced with HMRC's internal budget tracking.

DWP's August 2023 row is missing a GDP growth percentage. This does not affect the expenditure figures but means any analysis linking spending to economic conditions is incomplete for that month. The source data should be checked.

## Recommendation

Request a detailed breakdown of HMRC's August 2023 expenditure from the HMRC finance team, specifically identifying any one-off or exceptional items that month. Separately, chase the missing GDP figure with the economics team. Once both gaps are resolved, re-run this analysis to confirm the overall trend narrative holds.
